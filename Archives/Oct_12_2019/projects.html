Projects

Since 2018, I've been working with Dr. Jiook Cha in the Posner Lab at Columbia University where I have levered my passion for advanced math and machine learning to contribute to two really interesting research projects: 1) Create an algorithm to better analyze four-dimensional fRMI data and improve prescription efficacy for patients suffering from depression. 2) Examine complex brain imaging data in novel ways to better understand the effects of chronic stress on the HPA axis in humans.

MIT Think 2019 – Over the fall semester of my junior year, I and a partner developed an award winning proposal for the MIT Think competition in which we outlined an AI-based approach to addressing the shortage of trained health professionals in developing countries. Our proposal was one of a dozen in the nation earn semifinalist status.

Raspberry Pi – As a computer enthusiast, I wanted to learn how to make one myself. Using the raspberry pi kit, I built myself a miniature computer in a matter of weeks.

Line Following Robot – One of my first robotics projects was the creation of a line following robot. I built it from scratch at home over the summer.
